In William Gibson’s Neuromancer, reading, correspondence, and the opening of information are often mediated through technology, rendering traditional paper communication almost obsolete or "occult". References to these acts highlight the transition from physical to digital reality.

1. Reading as "Occult" Practice
The "Letter-Writers" in Chiba: Early in the novel, Case observes people using old voiceprinters wrapped in plastic in doorways, a nod to the "prestige" and almost forgotten nature of the written word in a digitized world.
Reading Data Structures: Case reads the "matrix" rather than text. The nonspace of the matrix allows him to read data constructs, where a small toy calculator might look like "limitless gulfs of nothingness" with a few basic commands. 
2. Correspondence and Data Transfer
ROM Constructs as Messages: The "Dixie Flatline" (McCoy Pauley) is a recorded, sentient ROM construct—a form of correspondence from a dead man. Reading or interacting with Dixie is a primary form of information gathering for Case.
Wintermute’s Communication: The AI Wintermute communicates through various channels, often taking over phone lines, computer displays, or manipulating people to act as messengers, forcing Case to interpret "messages" from the AI.
The "Screaming Fist" File: Armitage forces Case to read and access the data of his own past (the Screaming Fist run), treating memory as a file that can be opened and reviewed. 
3. Opening of Correspondence (Data/Information)
Breaking ICE (Intrusion Countermeasures Electronics): The most significant form of "opening correspondence" is hacking. Case "reads" data by breaking through digital security, described as traversing "seemingly abstract shapes and structures" to get to the information within.
Reading the "Meat Puppet" Data: When Case hooks into Molly's sensory feed, he is "reading" her experiences, effectively opening a live, direct feed of another person's consciousness and sensory input.
